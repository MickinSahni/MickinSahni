Getting Started.

December 8, 2015

Visited my cousin in San Fran. a couple months ago. He just moved there to work as a developer for a startup trying to change the way parents, teachers and students interact.

My goal: enjoy the people, the food and the city without thinking about work or unicorns. Lucky for me, we couldn't help it. We eventually got into where we're going to be in the future and how we're going to get there over one or two or five small-batch, locally-sourced, pour-over, vegan (?) coffees. (Best Coast)

He's an engineer and sees himself doing that for a while. I'm a "product developer" and I see myself doing that for a while. Why the quotes? Because everyone and no one knows what it means to be the product owner and/or manager and, because of that, find it hard to figure out how to do it well or at all.

That's where this project comes in. To help me (and perhaps others) strengthen the skills it takes to be a full-stack product developer, I'm going to gather my thoughts on data analysis, user experience, development and anything else I'll think of later.
